News

Stay current on education-sector threats.

Rather than re-publish headlines that go stale, Cyber Knights points you to the three sources the education community relies on most. Bookmark them, or check back here any time.

#1 Sector Specific

K12 SIX

The K-12 Security Information eXchange — a nonprofit threat-intelligence community dedicated solely to U.S. school district cybersecurity, publishing incident tracking and best-practice guidance.

k12six.org
#2 Government

CISA — Cybersecurity for K-12 Education

The U.S. Cybersecurity & Infrastructure Security Agency's dedicated K-12 hub: alerts, toolkits, and the "Protecting Our Future" guidance for school leaders.

cisa.gov
#3 Trade Press

K-12 Dive — Technology

Daily education-industry journalism covering ransomware, ed-tech vendor breaches, and federal policy moves affecting school cybersecurity.

k12dive.com

Cyber Knights links out to these independent, third-party sources for convenience and does not control their content. Opens in a new tab.

How to read a breach headline without panicking

Look for three things: what data was affected, whether it was your organization's own system or a third-party vendor, and what the source recommends doing next. Most education breaches trace back to phishing or vulnerable third-party software — both covered in our Knowledge Base and Best Practices sections.
