About the Project

Why Cyber Knights exists

Schools, colleges, and universities now hold large volumes of sensitive information — student records, exam data, staff payroll, and research material — and depend on digital platforms for teaching, communication, and administration. That dependence has made education an increasingly attractive target for cybercriminals, while leaving it among the least prepared sectors to defend itself.

Our Introduction

This site introduces the Cybersecurity Resource Hub, built by the Cyber Knights Team as a practical, easy-to-understand cybersecurity guide for the education sector. It combines short audio and video explainers with downloadable checklists so that teachers, administrators, IT staff, students, and parents can quickly understand common cyber risks in schools and take simple, actionable steps to reduce them.

The Evidence

Industry threat reports confirm this exposure is not theoretical. Zscaler ThreatLabz recorded a 35%+ year-over-year rise in education ransomware attacks, and the Centre for Internet Security found 82% of surveyed school districts experienced at least one cybersecurity incident in an 18-month period. Research also shows the core weakness isn't only technology — it's awareness and habit.

The gap we're closing

Limited Awareness

Many staff and students can't recognize phishing, weak passwords, or unsafe Wi-Fi use.

Budget Constraints

Schools rarely have dedicated cybersecurity teams or funding for awareness campaigns.

Scattered Information

Existing guidance is written for IT professionals and spread across too many sources.

Rising Consequences

Attacks cancel classes, delay exams, and expose student data for weeks at a time.

Our Mission

To design, build, and publish a live, shareable Cybersecurity Resource Hub that equips the education community with practical knowledge and tools — guided by the NIST Cybersecurity Framework's approach to identifying, protecting, detecting, responding to, and recovering from threats — translated into plain, non-technical language.

Summarize the most common school-facing cyber threats in plain language.

Publish an organized, easy-to-navigate public website.

Produce original audio/video explainers on key concepts.

Ship downloadable checklists to assess everyday cyber hygiene.
